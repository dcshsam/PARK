# SAP Proposal Evaluation Framework

Standard criteria for evaluating SAP proposals. Adjust weights to match the buyer’s priorities.

## Evaluation Criteria

| # | Criterion | Typical Weight | What to Look For |
|---|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| 1 | **Functional & Business Fit** | 20–30% | Coverage of required processes, industry-specific capabilities, fit-gap summary, standard vs. custom logic. |
| 2 | **Technical Architecture & SAP Alignment** | 15–25% | Clean-core strategy, extensibility model (BTP/RAP/CAP), integration design, security/identity, scalability. |
| 3 | **Implementation Methodology** | 10–15% | SAP Activate or equivalent phases, governance, agile/Waterfall mix, change management, training plan. |
| 4 | **Data Migration & Cutover** | 10–15% | Migration approach (greenfield/brownfield/landscape transformation), tooling, reconciliation, cutover plan, rollback. |
| 5 | **Team & Partner Qualifications** | 10–15% | Key personnel, certifications, industry experience, offshore/onshore mix, SME availability. |
| 6 | **Risk Management** | 5–10% | Risk register, mitigation plans, contingency, dependencies, assumptions clearly stated. |
| 7 | **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** | 10–20% | License, subscription, implementation, infrastructure, support, training, and ongoing run costs. |
| 8 | **Support & Hypercare** | 5–10% | Post-go-live support model, SLA, knowledge transfer, AMS transition, continuous improvement. |
| 9 | **Innovation & Differentiation** | 5–10% | AI/GenAI, automation, analytics, accelerators, reusable assets, future roadmap alignment. |
| 10 | **Compliance & Commercial Terms** | 5–10% | Contract structure, IP, liability, change control, exit clauses, data privacy, audit rights. |

## Scoring Scale

Use a consistent 1–5 scale:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Missing or seriously deficient |
| 2 | Weak, major gaps or concerns |
| 3 | Adequate, meets basic expectations |
| 4 | Good, exceeds in some areas |
| 5 | Excellent, clear differentiator |

## Weighted Score Calculation

For each criterion:

```
Weighted Score = Score × Weight%
Total Score = Sum of all Weighted Scores
```

Normalize weights so they sum to 100% within each review.

## Review Methods

- **Best-value tradeoff**: Balance technical merit and cost. Best for complex SAP transformations.
- **Lowest price technically acceptable (LPTA)**: Award to lowest compliant price. Only suitable when requirements are standardized and differentiation is minimal.

For most SAP projects, prefer best-value tradeoff because solution design, partner capability, and risk mitigation vary significantly.
